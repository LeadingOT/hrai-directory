# What is Interview Intelligence

**Interview Intelligence refers to the use of specialized software to capture, analyze, and derive insights from job interviews, transforming qualitative conversations into structured, actionable data.**

At its core, Interview Intelligence technology automates the post-interview workflow. It typically records and transcribes interviews (with participant consent), and then applies artificial intelligence—such as Natural Language Processing (NLP) and machine learning—to analyze the content. This goes beyond simple transcription to identify themes, evaluate responses against predefined competencies or job requirements, assess communication patterns, and even gauge sentiment. The output is not a replacement for human judgment, but a powerful augmentation tool that provides hiring teams with searchable transcripts, highlight reels, and data-driven summaries.

This technology matters because it directly addresses chronic challenges in hiring: inconsistency, bias, and poor candidate experience. By creating a standardized record of every interview, it reduces reliance on fallible human memory and fragmented notes. Hiring managers can compare candidate responses to the same question objectively, and recruiters can quickly pinpoint where a candidate discussed a specific skill. For example, a hiring team can ask the platform to show all moments where candidates explained a "failure and a lesson learned," allowing for a direct comparison. It also enables training and calibration; new interviewers can learn from examples, and teams can review their own questioning techniques.

Primary use cases include ensuring consistency in high-volume or panel interviews, reducing unconscious bias by focusing on response content rather than presentation, accelerating debriefs with shareable clips and data, and improving the overall quality of hire through more objective assessment. It is particularly valuable for roles where specific technical or behavioral competencies are critical to measure fairly.
