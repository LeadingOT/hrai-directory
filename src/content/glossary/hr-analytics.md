# What is HR Analytics

**HR Analytics is the systematic collection, analysis, and interpretation of people-related data to inform evidence-based decisions that improve workforce and business outcomes.**

HR Analytics, also known as people analytics or workforce analytics, moves beyond traditional HR reporting (which describes what happened) to diagnostic and predictive insights (explaining why it happened and what is likely to happen next). It applies statistical methods and data visualization to metrics such as employee turnover, recruitment efficiency, performance, engagement, and skills inventories. The goal is to transform raw HR data into actionable intelligence, linking people initiatives directly to key business performance indicators like productivity, profitability, and customer satisfaction.

The process typically involves defining a business problem, integrating data from various HR systems (e.g., ATS, HRIS, performance management, engagement surveys), cleaning and analyzing the data, and presenting findings to stakeholders. For example, instead of simply reporting a 15% annual turnover rate, HR Analytics would investigate which departments, managers, or employee cohorts have the highest risk of leaving, identify the leading predictors (e.g., lack of career path, low engagement scores, compensation benchmarks), and model the potential impact of targeted retention programs.

This analytical approach matters because it enables HR and business leaders to move from intuition-based to evidence-based management. Key use cases include predicting and reducing voluntary turnover, identifying characteristics of high-performing employees to refine hiring profiles, measuring the return on investment (ROI) of training programs, optimizing workforce planning, and uncovering root causes of low engagement. For instance, a company might use analytics to discover that employees who do not have a career conversation with their manager within their first six months are 40% more likely to leave within two years, leading to the implementation of a structured check-in program.
