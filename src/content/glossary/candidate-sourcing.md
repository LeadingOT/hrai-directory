# What is Candidate Sourcing

**Candidate sourcing is the proactive process of identifying, researching, and initiating contact with potential job candidates to build a pipeline of talent for current or future roles.**

Unlike reactive recruiting, which focuses on applicants who have already applied to a posted job, sourcing is an outbound, research-driven activity. Its primary goal is to discover and engage passive candidates—those who are not actively seeking a new job but possess the skills and experience an organization needs. Sourcers use a variety of tools and platforms, such as LinkedIn Recruiter, Boolean search strings on search engines, niche job boards, social media, and talent databases, to uncover and assess potential candidates. The process involves evaluating online profiles, portfolios, and public contributions (like code on GitHub or articles on industry sites) to qualify individuals before making the first contact, often via personalized outreach messages.

This initial stage of the recruitment funnel is critical for filling specialized, senior, or hard-to-fill positions where the ideal candidate is unlikely to be browsing job boards. Effective sourcing builds a sustainable talent pipeline, reducing time-to-hire and dependence on expensive agencies. It allows companies to be strategic, anticipating talent needs rather than scrambling when a vacancy opens. For example, a tech company seeking a rare expert in a specific AI framework would task a sourcer to find professionals who have published papers, contributed to relevant open-source projects, or spoken at conferences on that topic, then craft a tailored message to gauge their interest.

**Why it matters / Use cases:** Sourcing is essential for competitive hiring in talent-scarce markets. It is routinely used for executive search, recruiting for highly specialized technical roles (e.g., quantum computing engineers), and building diverse candidate pools by proactively seeking out talent from underrepresented groups. It transforms recruitment from a transactional process into a strategic talent acquisition function.
