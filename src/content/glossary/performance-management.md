# What is Performance Management

**Performance Management is a continuous, strategic process where managers and employees work together to plan, monitor, and review an employee's work objectives, overall contribution, and professional development.**

At its core, performance management shifts the focus from a once-a-year appraisal to an ongoing cycle of communication and feedback. The process typically begins with **planning and goal setting**, where individual objectives are aligned with team and company goals. This is followed by the **ongoing monitoring and coaching** phase, where regular check-ins provide opportunities for feedback, support, and adjustment. The cycle culminates in a **formal review and development discussion**, which assesses past performance and informs future goals and growth plans.

Modern performance management leverages technology and contemporary methodologies. Many organizations have moved away from rigid annual reviews to more agile frameworks, incorporating frequent **1-on-1 meetings**, **continuous feedback**, and **360-degree reviews**. Digital performance management tools are central to this, providing platforms to track Objectives and Key Results (OKRs), document feedback, and manage development plans efficiently. For example, a manager might use such a tool to log praise for a successful project immediately, rather than waiting months for a formal review.

Effective performance management matters because it directly links individual effort to organizational success. It provides employees with clarity on expectations, recognition for their work, and a clear path for growth, which boosts engagement and retention. For managers, it offers a structured framework for coaching and making informed decisions about promotions, compensation, and talent development. Common use cases include aligning a sales team around quarterly revenue targets, guiding a software developer's skill progression through regular code reviews and mentorship, or identifying high-potential employees for leadership programs.

**Related Terms:** 360-Degree Feedback, Objectives and Key Results (OKRs), Employee Engagement, Continuous Feedback, Talent Management, Performance Appraisal.
