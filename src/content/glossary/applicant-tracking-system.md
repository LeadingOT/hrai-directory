# What is Applicant Tracking System

**An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is a software application that automates the recruitment process by collecting, sorting, scanning, and ranking job applications.**

An ATS serves as a centralized digital hub for hiring. It begins by posting job openings to company career pages and multiple job boards. When candidates apply, the system stores every resume and application in a searchable database, replacing physical files and scattered email inboxes. Crucially, most ATS platforms parse resumes, extracting key information like skills, education, and work history to populate structured candidate profiles. This allows recruiters to filter and search for applicants based on specific criteria, dramatically reducing manual screening time. The system also manages communication, scheduling, and interview feedback, streamlining the entire hiring workflow from application to offer.

The core value of an ATS lies in its ability to manage high application volume and identify top candidates efficiently. For example, a recruiter with 500 applications for a software engineer role can use the ATS to instantly filter for candidates with "Python" and "5+ years experience," rather than reviewing each resume manually. It also ensures a consistent process, helps maintain compliance with hiring regulations, and provides a better experience for candidates through automated status updates.

**Why it matters / Use cases:** An ATS is essential for any organization seeking to scale hiring, improve quality-of-hire, and reduce time-to-fill. It is used by recruiters and hiring managers to eliminate administrative clutter, collaborate on candidates, and build a talent pipeline. Companies also leverage ATS data for reporting on hiring metrics like source effectiveness and diversity. For job seekers, understanding that an ATS scans resumes for keywords is critical to tailoring their applications to pass this initial screening.
