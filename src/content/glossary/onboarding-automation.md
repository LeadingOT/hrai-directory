# What is Onboarding Automation

**Onboarding automation is the use of software to streamline, manage, and execute the repetitive tasks and processes involved in integrating a new employee, customer, or user into an organization or platform.**

At its core, onboarding automation replaces manual, ad-hoc workflows with predefined, triggered sequences. For new employees, this means software can handle tasks like sending offer letters and paperwork, provisioning accounts (email, Slack, HRIS), scheduling training sessions, and assigning mentors—all without constant HR intervention. For customer or user onboarding, it automates welcome emails, in-app tutorials, milestone-based check-ins, and resource delivery based on user behavior. The automation platform acts as a central orchestrator, integrating with other tools (like HR systems, CRM, or project management software) to execute tasks and ensure nothing falls through the cracks.

The primary benefit is consistency and efficiency. Manual onboarding is prone to human error, delays, and variability in experience. Automation ensures every new hire or customer receives the same high-quality, timely introduction. It also frees up human resources (HR teams, customer success managers) from administrative burdens, allowing them to focus on high-touch, strategic interactions like personal welcome meetings or complex problem-solving. This shift from task-doer to experience-curator significantly enhances the quality of the onboarding journey.

**Why it matters / Use cases:**
Effective onboarding is critical for retention and productivity. For employees, a smooth, automated onboarding process reduces time-to-productivity, boosts engagement, and reinforces a positive company culture from day one. For customers, it directly impacts activation and reduces churn by helping them quickly realize the product's value. Key use cases include: automatically generating and tracking all new-hire documentation; building a 30-60-90 day plan with automated task assignments and reminders; creating customer onboarding sequences that trigger specific educational content after a user completes a key action; and ensuring IT asset management by automatically requesting laptop setup from the IT department.
